    What is “Fembooks”?

    "Fembooks" is the first feminist bookstore in the Chinese-speaking
    area, with the mission of promoting gender equality. After years of
    hard work, it was classified as a Taiwanese women's cultural landmark
    in 2007 and has become a place of pilgrimage for foreign tourists and
    those who care about gender issues. In addition to selling books, the
    Women's Bookstore also cooperates with elementary and middle schools
    to promote the "Gender-Friendly Dandelion Project". By donating gender
    equality education books and holding lectures, it takes root from an
    early age and enhances children's care and attention to more gender
    issues.

  2. Gender Friendly Dandelion Project

    "Fembooks" leverages its expertise in book selection to promote the "Campus
    Good Books Love Reading Gender-Friendly Dandelion" project, and cooperates
    with sponsors from all walks of life to donate gender education, gender
    identity and other related books to educational institutions at all levels,
    ranging from elementary schools to junior colleges. institutions, aiming
    to promote awareness of gender equality on campus. Schools that receive
    donated books will set up a "women's book section" in the library to place
    donated books and create a gender-friendly corner. In addition to donating
    books, the women's bookstore will also hold lectures or reading clubs in
    each school, with themes based on the needs of the school, such as children's
    human rights awareness, sexual equality laws and other issues.

  2. Tenderness of "Fembooks" in the Me Too incident

    During the Me Too movement that swept across Taiwan a while ago, other
    independent bookstores were busy removing books from the shelves and
    distancing themselves from controversial figures, but the women's bookstore
    was involved in the storm of public opinion and made different choices.

    "We are not the arbiters." Song Shunlian, the chairman of the female bookstore,
    said firmly. "Every book is written by the author with his life. Is it equated
    with his personal life and morality?" In this noisy era, everyone has something
    to say, but who says the "right" thing? Until the decision is made, we are not
    the arbiters, and neither is the female bookstore.

    "We are a space, and this space is for everyone to have a dialogue. I think this
    is what women's bookstores have been doing."

Sung, Shun-Lyn's
